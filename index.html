What I Do

Linguistics

articulatory phonetics | laboratory phonology | syntax

I am now MA student at the Grdaute Institue of Linguistics, National Taiwan University, Taipei, Taiwan. Paticularly, I am interested in ultrasound studies, articulatory and acoustic phonetics, and speech production and perception, and their interface. My thesis studies tonal coarticulation in Taiwan Mandarin and Taiwan Southern Min. Though seemingly a sound person, I also work with Prof. One-Soon Her of Tonghai University on Paiwan question classification.

Teaching

I take delight in teaching. I enjoy sharing what I know and learn what I don't in so doing. I have had multiple home tutoring experiences of teaching English and other subjects. I have also been the teaching assistant of a college English course and an introductory linguistics course.

Languages

I have always had enthusiasm for languages, which connect different people from different cultures. As a BA student, I majored in French and English in National Chenchi University, Taipei, Taiwan. It was also when I started to get to know linguistics, the scientific study of language.

Coding

My interest in languages goes beyond those of humans. I am a skilled Python coder. I am also familiar with R and Praat Script. And I am a newbie in Javascript and Swift. Coding not only helps with my linguistic research, but also has become one of my hobbies. Tis' always cool to see the computers do what you want them to do (and even more so when they don't)!
